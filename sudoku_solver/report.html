Algorithms & Heuristics
BT
Backtracking Search
Depth-first search over assignments. Backtracks when a contradiction is detected, exploring all valid completions systematically.
AC3
Arc Consistency (AC-3)
Propagates constraints through the constraint graph by iteratively removing values that have no support in peer domains.
FC
Forward Checking
After each assignment, removes the assigned value from all peers' domains immediately, catching contradictions early.
MRV
Min Remaining Values
Selects the unassigned variable with the fewest legal values — the "most constrained" cell — to assign next, pruning search branches.
DEG
Degree Heuristic
Tie-breaks MRV by choosing the variable involved in the most constraints on remaining unassigned variables.
LCV
Least Constraining Value
Orders the values to try for a variable by how few choices they eliminate from neighbouring unassigned cells.
Difficulty Analysis

Easy (1 backtrack, 9 ms): AC-3 pre-processing alone propagates most constraints to singleton domains. The solver barely needs to search — almost a direct solve.

Medium (777 backtracks, 1.9 s): Significantly more ambiguity. MRV and LCV keep the search focused, but the puzzle has more branching points that require genuine exploration.

Hard (5,275 backtracks, 12 s): The hardest puzzle in this set. Minimal given cells leave large unconstrained regions. The solver must commit to many speculative assignments before finding a consistent solution. This is a known hard benchmark for backtracking solvers.

Very Hard (330 backtracks, 658 ms): Interestingly fewer backtracks than Hard despite its reputation. This is the AI Escargot — the puzzle's structure happens to align well with MRV: few cells but strategically placed givens. A demonstration that "difficulty for humans" ≠ "difficulty for CSP solvers".
